## What we built

We built Arc Treasury as a yield vault on [Arc Network](https://arc.io). The idea is simple: you deposit stablecoins, and they earn real yield backed by US Treasury bills through USYC (US Yield Coin) by Hashnote. No inflationary token rewards, no ponzi mechanics. Just T-bill interest.

The app is live on Arc Testnet (chain ID `5042002`) at [arctreasury.biz](https://arctreasury.biz).

## How the yield works

Under the hood, we have a vault smart contract that does the following:

1. **Deposit** - you send USDC or EURC to the TreasuryVault contract
2. **Conversion** - the vault converts your stablecoins into USYC tokens via the USYC Teller
3. **Yield accrual** - USYC represents a share of a US Treasury bill fund managed by Hashnote. As T-bills accrue interest, the USYC price goes up
4. **Withdraw** - when you pull out, USYC converts back to your stablecoin at the current (higher) rate

The yield comes from actual US Treasury securities. Same instruments that traditional finance uses for capital preservation, just accessible through DeFi.

### Price oracle

We use an on-chain oracle (`USYCOracle`) that fetches the current USYC/USD exchange rate. It gets updated regularly and determines how many shares you receive on deposit and how much you get back on withdrawal.

## Supported stablecoins

We support two stablecoins:

| Token | Type | Decimals | Contract |
|-------|------|----------|----------|
| USDC | Native gas token | 18 | `0x360...000` |
| EURC | ERC-20 | 6 | `0x742b...4b69` |

One thing to note about Arc Network: USDC is the native gas token here (like ETH on Ethereum), so it has 18 decimal places instead of the usual 6. The vault handles all the decimal normalization internally, you don't need to think about it.

### Swapping between USDC and EURC

We also built an [AMM for swapping between USDC and EURC](https://arctreasury.biz/swap). The fee is 0.2% and the pool uses a constant product formula adjusted for the EUR/USD exchange rate.

## Wallet options

This is something we spent a lot of time on. We wanted the app to work for both crypto natives and people who have never touched a wallet before.

### MetaMask

Standard Web3 flow. Connect MetaMask or any compatible wallet, sign transactions yourself, full custody. Nothing new here.

### Google Sign-In

This is where it gets interesting. If you don't have a crypto wallet, just sign in with Google. We use Circle's Developer Controlled Wallets to create a managed wallet for you. Here's what happens:

- A wallet gets created automatically on your first sign-in
- Same address works on both Ethereum Sepolia and Arc Testnet
- All transactions go through Circle's API server-side
- No extensions, no seed phrases, no gas management

We think this is important for onboarding people who aren't crypto users yet.

## Bridging assets

To get USDC onto Arc Network, we built a [cross-chain bridge](https://arctreasury.biz/bridge) using Circle's CCTP V2:

- **Ethereum Sepolia to Arc Testnet** with automated attestation
- **Solana to Arc Testnet** for Solana users
- **Arc Testnet to Ethereum Sepolia** when you want to bridge back

The whole process takes a few minutes. Both MetaMask and Circle wallet users can use it.

## Locked positions and points

On top of standard (flexible) deposits, we have locked positions:

- **30 days** - bonus points multiplier
- **60 days** - higher multiplier
- **90 days** - highest multiplier

We track points on-chain and show them on the [rewards leaderboard](https://arctreasury.biz/rewards). You can get additional boosts from:

- **Early Supporter Badge** - an NFT that gives 1.2x points multiplier
- **Twitter verification** - connect your X account for a social boost
- **Referrals** - invite others and earn bonus points
